Significance in Quran and Hadith

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, and reflection. It commemorates the first revelation of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) in 610 AD.

In 624 AD (2 AH), fasting during this holy month was made obligatory for all adult Muslims. Beyond refraining from food and drink, it is a time for spiritual growth, increased charity, and practicing self-discipline.

"The month of Ramadan is that in which was revealed the Quran, a guidance for the people."

"Fasting is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, so that you may attain Taqwa." (Quran 2:183)

"Whoever fasts during Ramadan out of sincere faith and hoping to attain Allah's rewards, all his past sins will be forgiven." (Sahih Bukhari)

"The month of Ramadan is the one in which the Quran was revealed, a guidance for mankind." (Quran 2:185)

Spiritual Rewards (Sawab) in Ramadan

  • Fasting sincerely for Allah earns forgiveness of past sins. (Sahih Bukhari)
  • Charity (Sadaqah) in Ramadan multiplies rewards manifold. (Quran 2:261)
  • Night prayers (Tahajjud / Qiyam) bring closeness to Allah. (Sahih Muslim)
  • Reciting and reflecting on the Quran gives immense blessings. (Quran 2:185)
  • Showing kindness and helping others earns continuous rewards. (Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim)
